Why Do Gutters Become Clogged?.

  1. Fallen Leaves
  2. Organic Growth
  3. Small Animals
  4. Broken Tiles

When there is too much debris in the gutter it traps water, causing the gutter to become heavy and the joints mis-aligned. Excess rainwater will pour out of the sides of the gutter and on to the side of the house causing expensive repairs. Trapped water in the gutter can freeze in the winter causing damage to the gutter.

How Do I Clear The Gutters?

If your gutters are overflowing or leaking or if there is proof that water has run down the exterior of the building, it’s highly likely there is a blockage.

Contacting us to run a quick inspection will determine if there is a blockage and if so, we can usually clear the gutters for you, safely from the ground.

Using innovative industrial vacuums and a series of poles, professional gutter cleaning becomes safe and effective. With a one person operative, our systems can efficiently clear gutters safely from the ground to around 30ft. This means that we can reach into tricky gutter areas previously inaccessible with ladders such as over conservatories or garages.

 

How Do Blocked Gutters Cause Property Damage?

When gutters become blocked, rainwater is unable to flow which causes an overflow. This overflow creates a risk of expensive damage to walls, windows, fascia boards, ceilings, and possible damage to the foundations. There also becomes a serious risk of mould spores and dampness within the home which can cause illness.

Whichever gutter type the property has, regular maintenance will ensure they are free of debris and save on expensive repairs. Cast iron, pressed steel or plastic gutters – it is essential that these are maintained to ensure rainwater is free flowing.
